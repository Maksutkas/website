    Their entire hystory
    The history of motorcycles begins in the second half of the 19th century. The first self-propelled two-wheeled vehicles appeared in the 60s of the XIX century. The only power plant available at that time was a steam engine. However, the small size of the steam engine limited its power and speed.
    The first motorcycle with an internal combustion engine was built in 1885 by German engineers Gottlieb Daimler and Wilhelm Maybach. It was equipped with a 0.5-liter single-cylinder internal combustion engine and could reach speeds of up to 12 km/h.
    In the following years, motorcycles began to develop and improve. More powerful engines, better suspensions and brakes appeared. Motorcycles became more comfortable and safe.

    In the 20th century, motorcycles became a popular means of transportation. They were used for everyday travel, as well as for racing and other sporting events.
    In the 21st century, motorcycles continue to evolve. New technologies are emerging that make motorcycles even more powerful, faster and safer.
    All types of them
    Motorcycles can be classified according to various criteria. One of the most common ways to classify is by engine type. According to this criterion, motorcycles can be: Gasoline - the most common type of motorcycle. They are equipped with gasoline internal combustion engines. Gasoline motorcycle Electric - motorcycles powered by an electric motor. They are more environmentally friendly and quieter than gasoline motorcycles. Electric motorcycle Hybrid motorcycles are motorcycles equipped with a gasoline and an electric engine. They combine the advantages of gasoline and electric motorcycles. Hybrid motorcycle
